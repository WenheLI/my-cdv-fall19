Atari Shock

Atari shock was a large-scale recession in the video game industry, primarily in the United States.

The crash was attributed to several factors, including market saturation in the number of game consoles and available games, and waning interest in console games in favor of personal computers.

After this shock, the console game manufacturers adopt a more strict game quality check.
